### Key Takeaways

 To turn off silent mode on your iPhone, flip the switch on the side of your phone. You can also disable silent mode by enabling Assistive Touch, then tapping Device > Mute. 

 To turn off [silent mode](https://android-transfer.techidaily.com/how-to-transfer-data-from-vivo-y55s-5g-2023-to-blackberry-drfone-by-drfone-transfer-from-android-transfer-from-android/) and bring your iPhone back to ring mode, you can either use your phone's physical switch or use an option in the Settings app. We'll show you both ways to disable the silent mode.

 The easy way to turn off silent mode is to use the physical Ring/Silent switch. If this switch is broken or is faulty, then use an option in the Settings app to disable silent mode. In case your [phone's physical switch is stuck](https://video-capture.techidaily.com/in-2024-real-time-film-extractor/) on silent mode, the Settings option will override that and bring you back to ring mode.

##  Turn Off Silent Mode on iPhone Using the Switch

 On the left side of your iPhone, you have a small switch that you can flip to switch between ring and silent mode.

 If your iPhone is currently in silent mode, then behind this switch, you will see orange color.

![The orange on the silence switch is visible.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/orange-thing-showing.jpg) 

Nick Lewis / How-To Geek

 To disable silent mode and enable ring mode, flip this switch once so that you don't see the orange color anymore.

![The switch is set to off.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/flip-the-switch-to-black.jpg) 

Nick Lewis / How-To Geek

 Your iPhone is now in ring mode, and you're all set.

##  Take iPhone Off Silent Mode in Settings

 If you can't use the physical switch to deactivate silent mode, then use an option within Settings to go back to ring mode.

 To do so, first, open the Settings app on your iPhone. In Settings, tap "Accessibility." If you don't see this option, tap "General" and then tap "Accessibility."

![Tap "Accessibility" in the Settings app.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/open-accessibility.png)

##  Bonus Tip: Make a Shortcut to Turn Silent Mode On and Off

 If you use an iPhone 8 or later running iOS 14 or newer, you can [tap your phone's back](https://graphic-issues.techidaily.com/venturing-beyond-standard-definition-a-look-at-4k-tvs/) two or three times to enable and disable silent mode. You can activate this feature by heading into Settings > Accessibility > Touch > Back Tap and then choosing an appropriate option.

![Select the "Mute" option under "Double Tap" or "Triple Tap."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/09/mut.png)
