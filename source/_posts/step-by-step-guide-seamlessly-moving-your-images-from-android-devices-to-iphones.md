##  Transfer Photos from Android to iPhone Wirelessly

 The "Move to iOS" app works wirelessly, but it can only be used during the initial setup process---so we'll use [iCloud](https://facebook-video-share.techidaily.com/updated-in-2024-breaking-barriers-worlds-best-and-largest-shorts-downloaders/) as a secondary method. But wait...iCloud works on Android? We can sync photos from Android to iPhone through iCloud in the browser.

 First, head over to [icloud.com](http://icloud.com) in a browser on your Android device and sign in with the Apple ID for your iPhone.

![Go to icloud.com and "Sign In."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/2023-03-24_15-56-09.jpg) 

 Next, tap the grid icon in the top right and select "Photos."

![Tap the grid icon and choose "Photos."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/2023-03-24_15-58-06.png)

##  Transfer Photos from Android to iPhone With a Computer

 It's not necessary to use a computer to transfer photos from Android to iPhone, but it can be done if that's the method you prefer. The process is just a matter of dragging and dropping.

 First, connect your Android device to your Windows or Mac computer with a USB cable. Mac users will need to install the [Android File Transfer](https://www.android.com/filetransfer/) app. Photos and videos on an Android device are stored in DCIM > Camera.

![Navigate to the photos on Android device.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/2023-03-27_10-53-55.png)
