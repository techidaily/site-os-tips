##  Disable Fitness Tracking on Your iPhone

[Motion & Fitness tracking on your iPhone](https://unlock-android.techidaily.com/how-to-remove-or-bypass-knox-enrollment-service-on-honor-magic-v2-by-drfone-android/) utilizes the built-in accelerometer to detect and count your steps. It is an excellent feature, especially for those who don't have a fitness tracker. However, if you already have an Apple Watch, turning off iPhone's fitness tracking features should help you save some battery.

 It is important to clarify that turning the feature off doesn't impact your Apple Watch's accuracy, as both devices use their respective sensors. To disable Motion & Fitness tracking on your iPhone, open the Settings app.

![iPhone's home screen with an arrow pointing toward the settings app](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/1-6.png) 

 Scroll down to select "Privacy & Security."

![iPhone's settings app menu with an arrow next to the Privacy & Security settings option](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/2-6.png) 

 Then hit "Motion & Fitness."

![Privacy and security menu on an iPhone with an arrow next to the Motion & Fitness option](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/3-5.png) 

 Turn off the feature by pressing the toggle next to it.

![Motion & Fitness menu with a box around the Fitness Tracking option](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/4-3.png)

##  Disable Keyboard Haptics

 Thanks to iPhone's Taptic Engine, you get satisfying haptic feedback while typing on the keyboard. However, Apple notes that [keyboard haptics can affect iPhone battery life](https://support.apple.com/en-us/HT213404), especially if you spend a lot of time replying to emails or text messages. Consider disabling the keyboard's feedback to get more juice out of iPhone battery. Every little bit helps!

 Head to Settings and open "Sound & Haptics."

![Options in Settings app on an iPhone with an arrow next to the Sound & Haptics option](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2023/08/1-7.png)

##  Disable Automatic App Updates

 Most of us have many apps installed on our iPhones, and on any given day, one or more of them are likely to get an update. While it's [recommended to install these updates](https://sim-unlock.techidaily.com/in-2024-how-to-unlock-sim-cards-of-xiaomi-redmi-note-13-pro-5g-without-puk-codes-by-drfone-android/) to get the best experience and new features, with automatic updates enabled, your iPhone downloads and installs apps repeatedly. The extra work of downloading and installing the apps in the background uses up precious battery life.

 To disable automatic App Updates, open the Settings app on your iPhone and choose App Store.

##  Disable Live Activities

 An iOS 16 feature, Live Activities helps you keep track of important information on the Lock Screen. However, constantly getting data from apps and showing them as live widgets on the home screen is battery-intensive. If you're facing a severe battery drain issue, switch off Live Activities on your iPhone.

 Open the Settings app and scroll down to select "Face ID & Passcode." Enter your passcode on the following screen.

##  Avoid Using Your iPhone While it is Plugged in

 You might have noticed that your iPhone heats up while charging. This happens because the process of storing energy in the battery generates a significant amount of heat (especially when using a high-power rapid charger). Now, if you use your phone simultaneously, especially for heavy tasks, you add more heat into the system as both the phone's processors and the screen become warm with use.

 Constant exposure to heat or high temperature can permanently damage your iPhone's battery, [reducing its battery health](https://some-approaches.techidaily.com/the-verdict-on-vllo-performance-for-2024/). In the long run, this could decrease the total backup your iPhone provides. While it's not the end of the world, you might not want to develop the habit of using your iPhone when it's connected to the power adapter, particularly if you'd like to get years of life out of your iPhone's battery.
